FRG Activities

The following activities were organized as part of the AIM FRG:

  • A zoom workshop in summer 2020 organized by Brian Conrey (AIM) over Sococo and Zoom.

  • A research seminar (2020-2021) organized by Brian Conrey (AIM) over Sococo and Zoom.

  • A graduate seminar (2021-2024) organized by Anurag Sahay (Purdue) over Zoom.

  • An in-person workshop in summer 2021 organized by Emilia Alvarez (Bristol) at the AIM location in San Jose.

  • Two problem sessions over zoom; the first on March 23rd, 2021 moderated by Brian Conrey (AIM) and the second on April 22nd, 2024 moderated by Ofir Gorodetsky (Oxford).

  • Several working groups on themes related to the FRG, such as the \(\gamma_k(c)\) working group.

The following activities were not officially part of the FRG, but were organized by individuals associated with the FRG and/or dealt with related themes:

  • 50 Years of Number Theory and Random Matrix Theory Conference, held at the Institute of Advanced Study, Princeton in June 2022.

  • Random matrices from quantum chaos to the Riemann zeta function, held at the University of Bristol in July 2023.

  • PIMS CRG on L-functions in Analytic Number Theory (2022-2025).

A detailed record of the activities, including video links, can be found below.

2020 Workshop

This was a summer workshop organized by Brian Conrey; it was originally meant to be held in person, but was moved to Sococo and Zoom due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The workshop kicked off the activities of the FRG with several talks on the themes of the FRG, namely moments of L-functions and point-counting on varieties and helped familiarize the FRG members with the Sococo workspace.

Research Seminar

Originally called the “virtual FRG seminar”, this Zoom seminar was organized by Brian Conrey and was held on Tuesdays. It ran from August 2020 to June 2021 and served as the main weekly activity of the FRG at the time, usually featuring a 1.5 hour talk given by an FRG member on their research (or by a non-member on their own work related to the themes of the FRG). For a brief period, this was called the “Tuesday seminar” or the “adult seminar” to distinguish it from the graduate seminar (see below).

2021 Workshop

This was a summer workshop organized by Emilia Alvarez and held in the San Jose location of AIM. The primary focus of the workshop was the connection between moments of characteristic polynomials of random matrices and the Painlevé equations, though other topics (including the \(\gamma_k(c)\) function) were also discussed. Informal talks were given by Emilia Alvarez (Bristol), Siegfred Baluyot (AIM), Estelle Basor (AIM), Brian Conrey (AIM), Alexandra Florea (UC Irvine), Johannes Forkel (Oxford), Dan Goldston (SJSU), Matilde Lalín (Montréal), Andean Medjedovic (Waterloo), Brad Rodgers (Queen's), Michael Rubinstein (Waterloo), and others. Grad Seminar

This Zoom seminar was organized by Anurag Sahay and was held on Mondays. The seminar was originally meant to complement the “adult” or research seminar (see above) by providing the graduate students in the FRG a space to give expository talks on papers they were reading; during this period it was also called the “Monday seminar”. When the research seminar wound down, the scope of the seminar was expanded to include research talks by FRG members, and then later also to talks by external speakers, with the caveat that speakers were asked to make the talks accessible to graduate students and other early career researchers. The seminar ran from February 2021 to April 2024, and served as the main weekly activity of the FRG after October 2021. Notes and slides for talks (when available) are collected here.
